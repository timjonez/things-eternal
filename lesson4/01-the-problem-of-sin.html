— The Problem of Sin —

The result of Adam’s deliberate disobedience was the introduction of SIN into the human race. Because of our family-tree connection to Adam, we are all sinners. As a result, we commit sins.

Sin separates us from a holy God and ultimately keeps us from spending eternity with Him in Heaven. The question therefore becomes, “How can I escape sin’s awful consequences to have eternal life and a right relationship with my Creator?

  1. Adam’s attempts to cover his sin and shame:

    1. He tried to cover it up —

      Genesis 3:7 — “And the ___ of them both were opened, and they knew that they were naked; and they sewed fig leaves together, and made themselves ___.”

    2. He tried to shift the blame —

      Genesis 3:12 — “And the man said, The woman whom thou ___ to be with me, she gave me of the tree, and I did eat.”

  2. Man’s subsequent attempts to get right with God invariably involve “DOING:”

    For example:

    1. Matthew 19:16 — “And, behold, one came and said unto him, Good Master, what good thing shall I DO, that I may have eternal life?”

    2. Acts 16:30 — “And brought them out, and said, Sirs, what must I DO to be saved?”

  3. The best you could ever do is not good enough —

    Isaiah 64:6 — “But we are all as an ___ thing, and ALL our righteousnesses are as filthy rags; and we all do fade as a leaf; and our iniquities, like the wind, have ___ us away.”

  4. Good works will NEVER save anyone —

    Matthew 5:20 — “For I say unto you, That except ___ righteousness shall EXCEED the righteousness of the scribes and Pharisees, ye shall in no case enter into the kingdom of ___.”

    The epitome of a Pharisee: Philippians 3:4-7.

    1. Ephesians 2:8-9 — “For by ___ are ye saved through faith; and that not of yourselves: it is the ___ of God: NOT OF WORKS, lest any man should boast.”

    2. Titus 3:5-6 — “NOT BY ___ of righteousness which we have done, but according to his mercy he saved us, by the washing of regeneration, and renewing of the Holy ___; Which he shed on us ___ through Jesus Christ our Saviour.”

    3. Romans 10:2-3 — “For I ___ them record that they have a ZEAL of God, but not according to knowledge. For they being ___ of God's righteousness, and going about to establish their OWN ___, have not submitted themselves unto the righteousness of God.”

    4. Romans 3:20 — “Therefore by the DEEDS of the law [trying to keep the Ten Commandments] there shall NO flesh be justified in his sight: for by the law is the knowledge of sin.”

    5. Good works means human activities such as:

      1. Religion — baptism, church attendance, praying, giving.
      2. Community service — helping others, giving blood.
      3. Moral living — high standards, good manners.

      All these activities (and more) are “good” and commendable, but they are “works” — something that man does. The problem with them is they do not address the SIN issue. Good people will go to Hell if they die in their sins. Acts 10:1-2,5-6; John 8:21,24.
