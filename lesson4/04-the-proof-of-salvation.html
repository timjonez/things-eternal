  3. Christ’s resurrection assures our salvation —

    Acts 17:31 — “Because he hath appointed a day, in the which he will ___ the world in righteousness by that man whom he hath ordained; whereof he hath ___ ASSURANCE unto all men, in that he hath raised him from the dead.”

    If Jesus Christ had not risen from the dead, He would be no different from any dead founder of any of the world’s religions.

  4. Christ’s resurrection secures our justification —

    Romans 4:24-25 — “But for us also, to whom it [the righteousness of ___] shall be imputed, if we believe on him that raised up ___ our Lord from the dead; Who was delivered for our offences, and was ___ again for our justification.”

    1. Justification is a term of judicial standing before the Law.
    2. When God sees me in Christ, it is “just-if-ied” never sinned.

It is not enough that the eternal Son of God, Jesus Christ, died and rose again. His death and resurrection must be personally appropriated if we want to be saved. Christ died for the sins of the world (for every single human being) — but He is only the Saviour of those who believe.

“For therefore we both ___ and suffer reproach, because we trust in the ___ God, who is the Saviour of ALL men, SPECIALLY of those that believe” — I Thessalonians 4:10.
