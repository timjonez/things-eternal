Bible Study Five

Eternal Salvation

Key Verse: Hebrews 5:9.

“And being made perfect [Christ’s perfect obedience to the Father’s will], he became the author of eternal salvation unto all them that obey him.”

— The Meaning of Salvation Is “Deliverance” —

In order to study the subject of salvation, it is important to first understand what the Bible means by “salvation.”

To be “saved” means to be delivered! But, delivered from what?

Salvation delivers from:

  1. The CONSEQUENCES of SIN — II Corinthians 1:10.

    “Who DELIVERED us from so great a death [PAST], and doth DELIVER [PRESENT]: in whom we trust that he will yet DELIVER us [FUTURE].”

    Notice the past, present, and future aspects of salvation:

    1. PAST: deliverance from sin’s PENALTY — Romans 6:23a. “For the wages of sin is death...” Ezekiel 18:20.
    2. PRESENT: deliverance from sin’s POWER — Romans 6:14,18. “For sin shall not have dominion over you: for ye are not under the law, but under grace … Being then made free from sin, ye became the servants of righteousness.”
    3. FUTURE: deliverance from sin’s PRESENCE — I Corinthians 15:54. “So when this corruptible shall have put on incorruption, and this mortal shall have put on immortality, then shall be brought to pass the saying that is written, Death is swallowed up in victory.” Hebrews 9:28.

  2. The WRATH to Come — I Thessalonians 1:10.

    “And to wait for his Son from heaven, whom he raised from the dead, even Jesus, which DELIVERED us from the wrath to come.”

  3. The Power of DARKNESS — Colossians 1:13.

    “Who hath DELIVERED us from the power of darkness, and hath translated us into the kingdom of his dear Son.”

  4. The FEAR of DEATH — Hebrews 2:14-15.

    “Forasmuch then as the children are partakers of flesh and blood, he also himself likewise took part of the same; that through death he might destroy him that had the power of death, that is, the devil; And DELIVER them who through fear of death were all their lifetime subject to bondage.”

  5. The CORRUPTION of the WORLD SYSTEM — Galatians 1:4.

    “Who gave himself for our sins, that he might DELIVER us from this present evil world, according to the will of God and our Father.”

Salvation does not automatically mean deliverance from the consequences of sins that have been committed in the past. Very often the scars of sin remain — even though those sins have been forgiven.
