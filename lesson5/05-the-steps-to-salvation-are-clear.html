Bible Study Five

Eternal Salvation

Key Verse:

Hebrews 5:9 — “And being made perfect [___’s perfect obedience to the Father’s will], he became the ___ of eternal salvation unto all them that obey him.”

— The Steps to Salvation Are Clear —

What must occur in order for you to be saved the Bible way? Remember, the Lord Jesus Christ has done absolutely everything necessary to provide the gift of eternal life. So what must you do to receive that gift?

The answer is given in

Ephesians 2:8-9 — “For by grace are ye ___ through faith; and that not of yourselves: it is the gift of God: Not of ___, lest any man should boast.”

Salvation is BY GRACE … THROUGH FAITH … IN CHRIST.

  1. Salvation is by GRACE:

    1. “Grace” means “undeserved favor or kindness.”

      1. We all deserve the Lake of Fire —

        Romans 1:32 — “Who knowing the judgment of God, that they which commit such things are WORTHY OF DEATH, not only do the same, but have pleasure in them that do them.”

      2. God loves us in spite of our sin —

        Romans 5:8 — “But God commendeth his love toward us, in that, WHILE WE WERE YET SINNERS, Christ died for us.”

    2. God’s grace is exceeding abundant —

      Romans 5:20 — “Moreover the law entered, that the offence might abound. But where sin abounded, grace did much more abound.”

    3. Grace precludes any attempt to please or appease God.

      1. Grace and works are incompatible —

        Romans 11:6 — “And if by grace, then is it no more of works: otherwise grace is no more grace. But if it be of works, then is it no more grace: otherwise work is no more work.”

      2. Salvation is solely by God’s grace to us —

        II Timothy 1:9 — “Who hath saved us, and called us with an holy calling, not according to our works, but according to his own purpose and grace, which was given us in Christ Jesus before the world began.”

      3. God’s grace is FREE grace —

        Romans 3:24 — “Being justified FREELY by his grace through the redemption that is in Christ Jesus.”

    4. Receiving God’s grace requires humility —

      James 4:6 — “But he giveth more grace. Wherefore he saith, God resisteth the PROUD, but giveth grace unto the HUMBLE.”

  2. Salvation is through FAITH:

    “Faith” means believing God’s Word and acting upon it —

    Hebrews 11:1 — “Now ___ is the SUBSTANCE [reality] of things hoped for, the EVIDENCE [proof] of things not seen.”

    “Whom [Jesus Christ] ___ not seen, ye love; in whom, though now ye see him not, yet believing, ye rejoice with joy ___ and full of glory: ___ the end of your faith, even the salvation of your souls.” I Peter 1:8-9.

    “In hope of ___ life, which God, that cannot lie, promised before the ___ began.” Titus 1:2.

    In other words, faith is believing or accepting what GOD says about you (a lost Hell-bound sinner), what the Bible says about Jesus Christ and His death, burial, and resurrection, and what God’s Word says about the way of salvation — AND acting accordingly.

    1. How do I get faith?

      1. Romans 10:17 — “So then faith cometh by hearing, and hearing by the word of God.”

      2. I Thessalonians 2:13 — “For this cause also thank we God without ceasing, because, when ye received the word of God which ye heard of us, ye received it not as the word of men, but as it is in truth, the word of God, which effectually worketh also in you that believe.” Ephesians 1:13; Acts 17:11.

    2. What does it mean to BELIEVE?

      • II Timothy 1:12cd — “For I KNOW whom I have BELIEVED, and am PERSUADED that he is able to keep that which I have COMMITTED unto him against that day.”

      • Hebrews 11:13 — “These all died in faith, not having received the promises, but having SEEN them afar off, and were PERSUADED of them, and EMBRACED them, and confessed that they were strangers and pilgrims on the earth.”

      1. It first means to KNOW and understand what God says.

        • Acts 26:27 — “King Agrippa, believest thou the prophets? I know that thou believest.”

      2. It also means to be PERSUADED of what God says.

        • Acts 26:28 — “Then Agrippa said unto Paul, Almost thou persuadest me to be a Christian.”

      3. It then means you COMMIT your life to what God says.

        • Acts 26:29 — “And Paul said, I would to God, that not only thou, but also all that hear me this day, were both almost, and altogether such as I am, except these bonds.”

    3. What is the ACT of faith?

      Faith is not static; it is dynamic —

      • James 2:17-18 — “Even so faith, if it hath not works, is dead, being alone. Yea, a man may say, Thou hast faith, and I have works: shew me thy faith without thy works, and I will shew thee my faith by my WORKS.”

      • John 6:28-29 — “Then said they unto him, What shall we DO, that we might work the works of God? Jesus answered and said unto them, This is the WORK of God, that ye believe on him whom he hath sent.”

      1. Faith is an act of RECEIVING God’s GIFT —

        Romans 6:23 — “For the ___ of sin is death; but the GIFT of God is eternal life through Jesus Christ our Lord.”

        • A gift cannot be paid for; a gift is not a loan.
        • A gift must be accepted or received —

          John 1:12 — “But as many as RECEIVED him [Christ], to them gave he power to become the sons of God, even to them that BELIEVE on his name.”

      2. Faith is an act of TURNING — a “committal to;” an “embracing of” —

        I Thessalonians 1:9 — “For they themselves shew of us what ___ of entering in we had unto you, and how ye turned TO God FROM idols to serve the living and true God.”

        • The Bible word for this turning is “REPENTANCE.”
        • Repentance means “a change of mind that leads to a change in action.”
        • Repentance and faith are “two sides of one coin;” they are inseparable —

          Acts 20:21 — “Testifying both to the Jews, and also to the ___, repentance toward God, and faith toward our Lord ___ Christ.”

        • Repentance means a change of MIND —

          Job 42:6 — “Wherefore I ABHOR MYSELF, and repent in dust and ashes.”

          I Kings 8:47; Jeremiah 8:6; II Timothy 2:25.
        • Repentance means a godly (Godward) sorrow; not remorse —

          II Corinthians 7:10 — “For godly sorrow worketh repentance to salvation not to be repented of: but the sorrow of the world worketh death.”

        • Repentance means a change of ACTION or direction —

          Hebrews 6:1 — “Therefore leaving the principles of the doctrine of Christ, let us go on unto perfection; not laying again the foundation of repentance FROM DEAD WORKS, and of faith toward God.”

          Ezekiel 14:6; Matthew 3:8; Acts 26:20.

  3. Salvation is in CHRIST — it is “not of works.”

    1. Christ is the ONLY true Saviour for all men —

      I John 4:14 — “And we have seen and do testify that the Father sent the Son to be the Saviour of the world.”

    2. Salvation is Christ plus nothing, minus nothing:

      1. Romans 4:5 — “But to him that worketh not, but BELIEVETH ON HIM that justifieth the ungodly [the Lord Jesus Christ], his faith is counted for righteousness.”

      2. Galatians 5:4 — “Christ is become of NO effect unto you, whosoever of you are justified by the law; ye are fallen from grace.”

    3. There isn’t anything you can do to change your sin nature —

      Jeremiah 13:23 — “Can the Ethiopian change his skin, or the leopard his spots? then may ye also do good, that are accustomed to do evil.”

    4. To reject Christ is to reject eternal salvation —

      Acts 13:46 — “Then Paul and Barnabas waxed bold, and said, It was necessary that the word of God should first have been spoken to you: but seeing YE put it from you, and judge yourselves unworthy of everlasting life, lo, we turn to the Gentiles.”
