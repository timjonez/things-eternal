Bible Study One

The Eternal Supreme Being (God)

Key Verse: Deuteronomy 33:27ab

“The eternal God is thy refuge, and underneath are the everlasting arms…”

— Who and What God Is —

Allow God to speak for Himself!

There are numerous strange ideas (“imaginations”) about whom and what God is. Most organized religions have been developed by people who have put forward their own ideas about God.

Genesis 6:5; Romans 1:21-23; Jeremiah 13:10; Matthew 15:3,6,8-9; Colossians 2:8; I Peter 1:18.

It’s time to allow God to speak for Himself. The Bible is God speaking for Himself — John 5:39.

“Search the scriptures; for in them ye think ye have eternal life: and they are they which testify of me.”

The only way we can comprehend an infinite God with our finite minds is for the Bible to describe Him in terms of human attributes.

The Bible makes four statements about what God is:

1. God is a SPIRIT — John 4:24.

“God is a Spirit: and they that worship him must worship him in spirit and in truth.”

Let’s break this verse down:

  1. “GOD:”

    1. Not “gods” (plural).
    2. He is the one, true, and living God.
      • Deuteronomy 6:4 — “Hear, O Israel: The LORD our God is one LORD.”
      • Isaiah 45:5 — “I am the LORD, and there is none else, there is no God beside me.”
    3. He exists as three distinct, yet coequal, co-eternal, and co-essential Persons — I John 5:7.

      “For there are three that bear record in heaven, the Father, the Word [Jesus], and the Holy Ghost: and these three are one.”

      • God the FATHER — Romans 1:7d. “Grace to you and peace from God our Father.”
      • God the SON (Jesus Christ) — Hebrews 1:8. “But unto the SON he [God] saith, Thy throne, O GOD, is for ever and ever…” Isaiah 9:6; I Timothy 3:16; John 10:30,33.
      • God the HOLY SPIRIT — II Corinthians 3:17. “Now the Lord is that Spirit: and where the Spirit of the Lord is, there is liberty.”

    4. As the Supreme Being, He is:

      • All knowing — Romans 11:33. “O the depth of the riches both of the wisdom and knowledge of God! how unsearchable are his judgments, and his ways past finding out!” I John 3:20; Psalm 139:1-4.
      • All powerful — Jeremiah 32:17. “Ah Lord GOD! behold, thou hast made the heaven and the earth by thy great power and stretched out arm, and there is nothing too hard for thee.” Genesis 18:14a.

  2. God “IS:”

    1. He is not the “I was” or “I will be.”
    2. He is eternal — the “I AM.”

      Exodus 3:13-14 — “And Moses said unto God, Behold, when I come unto the children of Israel, and shall say unto them, The God of your fathers hath sent me unto you; and they shall say to me, What is his name? what shall I say unto them? And God said unto Moses, I AM THAT I AM: and he said, Thus shalt thou say unto the children of Israel, I AM hath sent me unto you.” Psalm 90:2.

    3. He is the “first and the last” — Isaiah 44:6.

      “Thus saith the LORD the King of Israel, and his redeemer the LORD of hosts; I am the first, and I am the last; and beside me there is no God.”

      Isaiah 48:12-13; Revelation 1:8.

    4. He is unchanging and unchangeable — Malachi 3:6a. “For I am the LORD, I change not…”
    5. He is life — John 5:26. “For as the Father hath life in himself; so hath he given to the Son to have life in himself.”
    6. He is the Giver of life — Acts 17:24-25.

      “God that made the world and all things therein, seeing that he is Lord of heaven and earth, dwelleth not in temples made with hands; Neither is worshipped with men's hands, as though he needed any thing, seeing he giveth to all life, and breath, and all things.”

  3. God is a “SPIRIT:”

    This means:

    1. God is not a man — He possesses no material body.

      Numbers 23:19 — “God is not a man, that he should lie; neither the son of man, that he should repent: hath he said, and shall he not do it? or hath he spoken, and shall he not make it good?”

    2. God is invisible — I Timothy 1:17. “Now unto the King eternal, immortal, invisible, the only wise God, be honour and glory for ever and ever.”
    3. God is unlimited — I Kings 8:27. “But will God indeed dwell on the earth? behold, the heaven and heaven of heavens cannot contain thee; how much less this house that I have builded?” Psalm 139:7; Jeremiah 23:24.

  4. God is a PERSON — “they that worship HIM” (not “it”).

    Personality means God possesses:

    1. Intellect (intelligence) — He “KNOWS” (e.g., Exodus 3:7).

      “And the LORD said, I have surely seen the affliction of my people which are in Egypt, and have heard their cry by reason of their taskmasters; for I know their sorrows.”

    2. Sensibility (understanding; emotion) — He “FEELS” and cares (e.g., Exodus 2:24-25).

      “And God heard their groaning, and God remembered his covenant with Abraham, with Isaac, and with Jacob. And God looked upon the children of Israel, and God had respect unto them.”

    3. Volition (will; determination; purpose) — He “WILLS” (e.g., Exodus 3:17,20-21).

      “And I have said, I will bring you up out of the affliction of Egypt unto the land of the Canaanites, and the Hittites, and the Amorites, and the Perizzites, and the Hivites, and the Jebusites, unto a land flowing with milk and honey … And I will stretch out my hand, and smite Egypt with all my wonders which I will do in the midst thereof: and after that he will let you go. And I will give this people favour in the sight of the Egyptians: and it shall come to pass, that, when ye go, ye shall not go empty.”

    These essential traits of personality are infinite with God (in contrast to man’s finite or limited intelligence, understanding, emotions, will and determination.)

2. God is LIGHT — I John 1:5.

“This then is the message which we have heard of him, and declare unto you, that God is light, and in him is no darkness at all.”

  1. Darkness is a metaphor for evil — Proverbs 4:19.

    “The way of the wicked is as darkness: they know not at what they stumble.”

    Ephesians 5:11; II Corinthians 6:14; Matthew 4:16; Matthew 6:23; I John 1:6; I John 2:11; John 12:35.

  2. God is pure light — I Timothy 6:16.

    “Who only hath immortality, dwelling in the light which no man can approach unto; whom no man hath seen, nor can see: to whom be honour and power everlasting.”

    1. This means God is HOLY — Isaiah 6:1-3.

      “In the year that king Uzziah died I saw also the Lord sitting upon a throne, high and lifted up, and his train filled the temple. Above it stood the seraphims: each one had six wings; with twain he covered his face, and with twain he covered his feet, and with twain he did fly. And one cried unto another, and said, HOLY, HOLY, HOLY, is the LORD of hosts: the whole earth is full of his glory.”

      Isaiah 57:15; Psalm 145:17; Revelation 4:8; I Peter 1:16.

    2. Holiness means God is infinitely set apart from evil — Habakkuk 1:13a. “Thou art of purer eyes than to behold evil, and canst not look on iniquity…”
    3. Darkness cannot exist in the presence of pure Light — Ephesians 5:13.

      “But all things that are reproved are made manifest by the light: for whatsoever doth make manifest is light.”

      John 3:20-21.

  3. God is infinitely TRUE — Deuteronomy 32:4.

    “He is the Rock, his work is perfect: for all his ways are judgment: a God of truth and without iniquity, just and right is he.”

3. God is LOVE — I John 4:8b.

“He that loveth not knoweth not God; for God is love.”

  1. God does not merely love — He IS love.

  2. Love means the unconditional giving of oneself to another — regardless of the cost.

    1. Love is sacrificial — I John 3:16ab. “Hereby perceive we the love of God, because he laid down his life for us…”
    2. Love is unconditional — Deuteronomy 7:6-8.

      “For thou art an holy people unto the LORD thy God: the LORD thy God hath chosen thee to be a special people unto himself, above all people that are upon the face of the earth. The LORD did not set his love upon you, nor choose you, because ye were more in number than any people; for ye were the fewest of all people: But because the LORD loved you, and because he would keep the oath which he had sworn unto your fathers, hath the LORD brought you out with a mighty hand, and redeemed you out of the house of bondmen, from the hand of Pharaoh king of Egypt.”

    3. God’s love is everlasting — Jeremiah 31:3. “The LORD hath appeared of old unto me, saying, Yea, I have loved thee with an everlasting love: therefore with lovingkindness have I drawn thee.”
    4. God’s love is infinite — Ephesians 3:17-19.

      “That Christ may dwell in your hearts by faith; that ye, being rooted and grounded in love, May be able to comprehend with all saints what is the breadth, and length, and depth, and height; And to know the love of Christ, which passeth knowledge, that ye might be filled with all the fulness of God.”

  3. Love must have an object — John 3:16.

    “For God so loved the world, that he gave his only begotten Son, that whosoever believeth in him should not perish, but have everlasting life.”

  4. God’s love is not arbitrary; it cannot overrule God’s holiness. A loving God will never overlook or excuse evil.

4. God is a CONSUMING FIRE — Hebrews 12:29.

“For our God is a consuming fire.”

Exodus 24:17; Deuteronomy 4:24; Isaiah 66:15; II Thessalonians 1:8-9.

  1. God is the Judge — Genesis 18:25e. “Shall not the Judge of all the earth do right?”
  2. God is righteous in His judgments — Psalm 9:7-8.

    “But the LORD shall endure for ever: he hath prepared his throne for judgment. And he shall judge the world in righteousness, he shall minister judgment to the people in uprightness.”

    Psalm 50:6.

  3. God is Jealous for His Name and honour — Exodus 34:14. “For thou shalt worship no other god: for the LORD, whose name is Jealous, is a jealous God.” Isaiah 42:8.
