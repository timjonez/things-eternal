“Things Eternal”

“Things Eternal” is a five-part Bible study series covering topics that really matter; that do make a difference. Why are they so important? Because they deal with eternal (forever) issues!

Our life here on planet earth is finite; it will end at some point. Therefore, the most important question is, “Where will you spend eternity?”

“Whereas ye know not what shall be on the morrow. For what is your life? It is even a vapour, that appeareth for a little time, and then vanisheth away” — James 4:14.

I Peter 1:24; Job 7:6-7; Job 14:1-2; Psalm 39:4-6; Psalm 89:47-48; Psalm 90:10; Luke 12:19-20; I Timothy 6:7.

This Bible study series will introduce you to the eternal SUPREME BEING (God), Who has spoken to all mankind through the eternal SCRIPTURES (the Bible), that tells us of God’s eternal SON (Jesus Christ), through Whom we can have an eternal SALVATION that saves and keeps our everlasting SOUL.

Bible Study Five

Eternal Salvation

Key Verse: Hebrews 5:9.

“And being made perfect [Christ’s perfect obedience to the Father’s will], he became the author of eternal salvation unto all them that obey him.”

— The Meaning of Salvation Is “Deliverance” —

In order to study the subject of salvation, it is important to first understand what the Bible means by “salvation.”

To be “saved” means to be delivered! But, delivered from what?

Salvation delivers from:

  1. The CONSEQUENCES of SIN — II Corinthians 1:10.

    “Who DELIVERED us from so great a death [PAST], and doth DELIVER [PRESENT]: in whom we trust that he will yet DELIVER us [FUTURE].”

    Notice the past, present, and future aspects of salvation:

    1. PAST: deliverance from sin’s PENALTY — Romans 6:23a. “For the wages of sin is death...” Ezekiel 18:20.
    2. PRESENT: deliverance from sin’s POWER — Romans 6:14,18. “For sin shall not have dominion over you: for ye are not under the law, but under grace … Being then made free from sin, ye became the servants of righteousness.”
    3. FUTURE: deliverance from sin’s PRESENCE — I Corinthians 15:54. “So when this corruptible shall have put on incorruption, and this mortal shall have put on immortality, then shall be brought to pass the saying that is written, Death is swallowed up in victory.” Hebrews 9:28.

  2. The WRATH to Come — I Thessalonians 1:10.

    “And to wait for his Son from heaven, whom he raised from the dead, even Jesus, which DELIVERED us from the wrath to come.”

  3. The Power of DARKNESS — Colossians 1:13.

    “Who hath DELIVERED us from the power of darkness, and hath translated us into the kingdom of his dear Son.”

  4. The FEAR of DEATH — Hebrews 2:14-15.

    “Forasmuch then as the children are partakers of flesh and blood, he also himself likewise took part of the same; that through death he might destroy him that had the power of death, that is, the devil; And DELIVER them who through fear of death were all their lifetime subject to bondage.”

  5. The CORRUPTION of the WORLD SYSTEM — Galatians 1:4.

    “Who gave himself for our sins, that he might DELIVER us from this present evil world, according to the will of God and our Father.”

Salvation does not automatically mean deliverance from the consequences of sins that have been committed in the past. Very often the scars of sin remain — even though those sins have been forgiven.

— The Effect of Salvation Is Life-Changing —

Salvation is a supernatural work of GOD, whereby the sinner is BORN AGAIN. To be “born again” means to be made spiritually alive!

Another Bible word for the new birth is “regeneration.”

  • John 3:3-8 — “Jesus answered and said unto him, Verily, verily, I say unto thee, Except a man be born again, he cannot see the kingdom of God. Nicodemus saith unto him, How can a man be born when he is old? can he enter the second time into his mother's womb, and be born? Jesus answered, Verily, verily, I say unto thee, Except a man be born of water [physical birth] AND of the Spirit [spiritual birth], he cannot enter into the kingdom of God. That which is born of the flesh is flesh; and that which is born of the Spirit is spirit. Marvel not that I said unto thee, Ye must be born again. The wind bloweth where it listeth, and thou hearest the sound thereof, but canst not tell whence it cometh, and whither it goeth: so is every one that is born of the Spirit.”
  • Ephesians 2:1,5 — “And you hath he quickened [made spiritually alive], who were dead in trespasses and sins … Even when we were dead in sins, hath quickened us together with Christ, (by grace ye are saved).” John 6:63; Titus 3:5-6.

Just as we had nothing to do with our first (natural) birth, we have nothing to do with the second (spiritual birth). It is a work of God the Holy Spirit.

Through the new birth, God:

  1. Imparts a NEW NATURE — His nature.

    1. II Peter 1:3-4 — “According as his divine power hath given unto us all things that pertain unto life and godliness, through the knowledge of him that hath called us to glory and virtue: Whereby are given unto us exceeding great and precious promises: that by these ye might be partakers of the DIVINE NATURE, having escaped the corruption that is in the world through lust.”
    2. I John 3:9 — “Whosoever is born of God doth not commit sin; for HIS seed [the divine nature] remaineth in him: and he cannot sin, because he is born of God.”

  2. Imparts NEW LIFE — Colossians 3:4.

    “When Christ, WHO IS OUR LIFE, shall appear, then shall ye also appear with him in glory.”

    Galatians 2:20.

  3. Makes us a NEW CREATURE — II Corinthians 5:17.

    “Therefore if any man be in Christ, he is a new creature: old things are passed away; behold, all things are become new.”

    Galatians 6:15.

God doesn’t seek to “re-work” the old you. He will not do a makeover of your old life — it is spiritually dead, completely evil, and worthless.

  • John 3:6a — “That which is born of the flesh is FLESH...”
  • Romans 7:18 — “For I know that in me (that is, in my FLESH,) [the old, Adamic nature] dwelleth no good thing.”

— The Offer of Salvation Is Inclusive —

God’s gift of eternal salvation is offered to everyone; no exclusions!

  1. Salvation is for “WHOSOEVER WILL:”

    1. John 3:16 — “For God so loved the world, that he gave his only begotten Son, that WHOSOEVER believeth in him should not perish, but have everlasting life.”
    2. Revelation 22:17 — “And the Spirit and the bride say, Come. And let him that heareth say, Come. And let him that is athirst come. And WHOSOEVER WILL, let him take the water of life freely.”

  2. Salvation is for “ALL” who will come to Christ:

    1. I Timothy 2:3-4 — “For this is good and acceptable in the sight of God our Saviour; Who will have ALL men to be saved, and to come unto the knowledge of the truth.”
    2. II Peter 3:9 — “The Lord is not slack concerning his promise, as some men count slackness; but is longsuffering to us-ward, not willing that ANY should perish, but that ALL should come to repentance.”
    3. Titus 2:11 — “For the grace of God that bringeth salvation hath appeared to ALL men.” Ezekiel 18:22.

— The Way to Salvation Is Exclusive —

There is only ONE way to be saved, and that is through the Lord Jesus Christ.

  • John 14:6 — “Jesus saith unto him, I am THE way, THE truth, and THE life: no man cometh unto the Father, but by ME.”
  • John 10:9 — “I am THE door: by me if any man enter in, he shall be saved, and shall go in and out, and find pasture.”
  • Acts 4:12 — “Neither is there salvation in any other: for there is NONE OTHER name under heaven given among men, whereby we must be saved.”
  • Isaiah 45:22 — “Look unto me, and be ye saved, all the ends of the earth: for I am God, and THERE IS NONE ELSE.”
  • John 8:24 — “I said therefore unto you, that ye shall die in your sins: for if ye believe not that I am he, ye shall die in your sins.” Matthew 7:13-14.
  1. Being a “good person” will not save you — Romans 3:10. “As it is written, There is NONE righteous, no, NOT ONE.”
  2. Being a religious person will not save you — Matthew 7:21-23.

    “Not every one that saith unto me, Lord, Lord, shall enter into the kingdom of heaven; but he that doeth the will of my Father which is in heaven. Many will say to me in that day, Lord, Lord, have we not prophesied in thy name? and in thy name have cast out devils? and in thy name done many wonderful works? And then will I profess unto them, I never knew you: depart from me, ye that work iniquity.”

    Acts 10:1-6; II Timothy 3:5; Titus 1:16.

— The Steps to Salvation Are Clear —

What must occur in order for you to be saved the Bible way? Remember, the Lord Jesus Christ has done absolutely everything necessary to provide the gift of eternal life. So what must you do to receive that gift?

The answer is given in Ephesians 2:8-9.

“For by grace are ye saved through faith; and that not of yourselves: it is the gift of God: Not of works, lest any man should boast.”

Salvation is BY GRACE … THROUGH FAITH … IN CHRIST.

  1. Salvation is by GRACE:

    1. “Grace” means “undeserved favor or kindness.”

      1. We all deserve the Lake of Fire — Romans 1:32. “Who knowing the judgment of God, that they which commit such things are WORTHY OF DEATH, not only do the same, but have pleasure in them that do them.”
      2. God loves us in spite of our sin — Romans 5:8. “But God commendeth his love toward us, in that, WHILE WE WERE YET SINNERS, Christ died for us.”
    2. God’s grace is exceeding abundant — Romans 5:20. “Moreover the law entered, that the offence might abound. But where sin abounded, grace did much more abound.”

    3. Grace precludes any attempt to please or appease God.

      1. Grace and works are incompatible — Romans 11:6. “And if by grace, then is it no more of works: otherwise grace is no more grace. But if it be of works, then is it no more grace: otherwise work is no more work.”
      2. Salvation is solely by God’s grace to us — II Timothy 1:9. “Who hath saved us, and called us with an holy calling, not according to our works, but according to his own purpose and grace, which was given us in Christ Jesus before the world began.”
      3. God’s grace is FREE grace — Romans 3:24. “Being justified FREELY by his grace through the redemption that is in Christ Jesus.”
    4. Receiving God’s grace requires humility — James 4:6. “But he giveth more grace. Wherefore he saith, God resisteth the PROUD, but giveth grace unto the HUMBLE.”

  2. Salvation is through FAITH:

    “Faith” means believing God’s Word and acting upon it — Hebrews 11:1.

    “Now faith is the SUBSTANCE [reality] of things hoped for, the EVIDENCE [proof] of things not seen.”

    “Whom [Jesus Christ] having not seen, ye love; in whom, though now ye see him not, yet believing, ye rejoice with joy unspeakable and full of glory: Receiving the end of your faith, even the salvation of your souls.” I Peter 1:8-9.

    “In hope of eternal life, which God, that cannot lie, promised before the world began.” Titus 1:2.

    In other words, faith is believing or accepting what GOD says about you (a lost Hell-bound sinner), what the Bible says about Jesus Christ and His death, burial, and resurrection, and what God’s Word says about the way of salvation — AND acting accordingly.

    1. How do I get faith?

      1. Romans 10:17 — “So then faith cometh by hearing, and hearing by the word of God.”
      2. I Thessalonians 2:13 — “For this cause also thank we God without ceasing, because, when ye received the word of God which ye heard of us, ye received it not as the word of men, but as it is in truth, the word of God, which effectually worketh also in you that believe.” Ephesians 1:13; Acts 17:11.

    2. What does it mean to BELIEVE?

      • II Timothy 1:12cd — “For I KNOW whom I have BELIEVED, and am PERSUADED that he is able to keep that which I have COMMITTED unto him against that day.”
      • Hebrews 11:13 — “These all died in faith, not having received the promises, but having SEEN them afar off, and were PERSUADED of them, and EMBRACED them, and confessed that they were strangers and pilgrims on the earth.”
      1. It first means to KNOW and understand what God says.
        • Acts 26:27 — “King Agrippa, believest thou the prophets? I know that thou believest.”
      2. It also means to be PERSUADED of what God says.
        • Acts 26:28 — “Then Agrippa said unto Paul, Almost thou persuadest me to be a Christian.”
      3. It then means you COMMIT your life to what God says.
        • Acts 26:29 — “And Paul said, I would to God, that not only thou, but also all that hear me this day, were both almost, and altogether such as I am, except these bonds.”

    3. What is the ACT of faith?

      Faith is not static; it is dynamic — James 2:17-18.

      • “Even so faith, if it hath not works, is dead, being alone. Yea, a man may say, Thou hast faith, and I have works: shew me thy faith without thy works, and I will shew thee my faith by my WORKS.”
      • John 6:28-29 — “Then said they unto him, What shall we DO, that we might work the works of God? Jesus answered and said unto them, This is the WORK of God, that ye believe on him whom he hath sent.”

      1. Faith is an act of RECEIVING God’s GIFT — Romans 6:23.

        “For the wages of sin is death; but the GIFT of God is eternal life through Jesus Christ our Lord.”

        • A gift cannot be paid for; a gift is not a loan.
        • A gift must be accepted or received — John 1:12.

          “But as many as RECEIVED him [Christ], to them gave he power to become the sons of God, even to them that BELIEVE on his name.”

      2. Faith is an act of TURNING — a “committal to;” an “embracing of” — I Thessalonians 1:9.

        “For they themselves shew of us what manner of entering in we had unto you, and how ye turned TO God FROM idols to serve the living and true God.”

        • The Bible word for this turning is “REPENTANCE.”
        • Repentance means “a change of mind that leads to a change in action.”
        • Repentance and faith are “two sides of one coin;” they are inseparable — Acts 20:21.

          “Testifying both to the Jews, and also to the Greeks, repentance toward God, and faith toward our Lord Jesus Christ.”

        • Repentance means a change of MIND — Job 42:6. “Wherefore I ABHOR MYSELF, and repent in dust and ashes.” I Kings 8:47; Jeremiah 8:6; II Timothy 2:25.
        • Repentance means a godly (Godward) sorrow; not remorse — II Corinthians 7:10. “For godly sorrow worketh repentance to salvation not to be repented of: but the sorrow of the world worketh death.”
        • Repentance means a change of ACTION or direction — Hebrews 6:1. “Therefore leaving the principles of the doctrine of Christ, let us go on unto perfection; not laying again the foundation of repentance FROM DEAD WORKS, and of faith toward God.” Ezekiel 14:6; Matthew 3:8; Acts 26:20.

  3. Salvation is in CHRIST — it is “not of works.”

    1. Christ is the ONLY true Saviour for all men — I John 4:14. “And we have seen and do testify that the Father sent the Son to be the Saviour of the world.”

    2. Salvation is Christ plus nothing, minus nothing:

      1. Romans 4:5 — “But to him that worketh not, but BELIEVETH ON HIM that justifieth the ungodly [the Lord Jesus Christ], his faith is counted for righteousness.”
      2. Galatians 5:4 — “Christ is become of NO effect unto you, whosoever of you are justified by the law; ye are fallen from grace.”
    3. There isn’t anything you can do to change your sin nature — Jeremiah 13:23. “Can the Ethiopian change his skin, or the leopard his spots? then may ye also do good, that are accustomed to do evil.”
    4. To reject Christ is to reject eternal salvation — Acts 13:46. “Then Paul and Barnabas waxed bold, and said, It was necessary that the word of God should first have been spoken to you: but seeing YE put it from you, and judge yourselves unworthy of everlasting life, lo, we turn to the Gentiles.”

— The Duration of Salvation Is Forever —

  1. Salvation is EVERLASTING life — John 5:24.

    “Verily, verily, I say unto you, He that heareth my word, and believeth on him that sent me, hath EVERLASTING life, and shall not come into condemnation; but is passed from death unto life.”

  2. A believer is kept by the POWER of GOD — I Peter 1:5. “Who are kept by the power of God through faith unto salvation ready to be revealed in the last time.”
  3. A believer is secure in the HANDS of GOD — John 10:28-29. “And I give unto them eternal life; and they shall NEVER perish, neither shall any man pluck them out of my hand. My Father, which gave them me, is greater than all; and no man is able to pluck them out of my Father's hand.”
  4. A believer rests in the PROMISE of GOD — Philippians 1:6. “Being confident of this very thing, that he which hath begun a good work in you will perform it until the day of Jesus Christ.”
  5. A believer is PRESERVED and KEPT by GOD — Jude 1,24. “Jude, the servant of Jesus Christ, and brother of James, to them that are sanctified by God the Father, and PRESERVED in Jesus Christ, and called … Now unto him that is able to KEEP YOU from falling, and to present you faultless before the presence of his glory with exceeding joy.”

It is time for you to do business with God!

You’ve come to a “line of decision.” What will you do with Jesus Christ?

“Now the just shall live by faith: but if any man draw back [decide “NO”], my soul shall have no pleasure in him. But we are not of them who draw back unto perdition [Hell]; but of them that believe [decide “YES”] to the saving of the soul.” Hebrews 10:38-39

— God Wants to Hear from You —

We have studied the Bible to learn Who God is and what God has done for you through the Lord Jesus Christ. Jesus Christ has done everything necessary to save you from your sins and give you eternal life.

The gracious invitation to be saved has been given — it is time for you to RSVP. What will your answer be? The decision is yours to make.

“That if thou [YOU] shalt confess with thy mouth the Lord Jesus, and shalt believe in thine heart that God hath raised him from the dead, thou SHALT be saved. For with the heart man believeth unto righteousness; and with the mouth confession is made unto salvation.” Romans 10:9-10

  1. Confess with the MOUTH — talk to God.

    1. Confess (admit) your condition — Luke 18:13.

      “And the publican, standing afar off, would not lift up so much as his eyes unto heaven, but smote upon his breast, saying, God be merciful to me a sinner.”

      Psalm 51:3-4; Luke 15:21; I Timothy 1:15.

    2. Confess (acknowledge) your need — John 4:15. “The woman saith unto him, Sir, give me this water [the water of life], that I thirst not, neither come hither to draw.”
    3. Confess your total dependence upon Christ — John 6:68-69.

      “Then Simon Peter answered him, Lord, to whom shall we go? thou hast the words of eternal life. And we believe and are sure that thou art that Christ, the Son of the living God.”

      Psalm 73:25; Matthew 18:3-4.

    4. ASK Christ to save you — Romans 10:13. “For whosoever shall call upon the name of the Lord shall be saved.”

  2. Believe in the HEART — not merely the head.

    1. Believe what the Bible says Who Jesus IS — Acts 8:37. “And Philip said, If thou believest with all thine heart, thou mayest. And he answered and said, I believe that Jesus Christ is the Son of God.”
    2. Believe what the Bible says Jesus has DONE — I Peter 1:21. “Who by him do believe in God, that raised him up from the dead, and gave him glory; that your faith and hope might be in God.”
