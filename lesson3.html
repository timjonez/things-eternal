“Things Eternal”

“Things Eternal” is a five-part Bible study series covering topics that really matter; that do make a difference. Why are they so important? Because they deal with eternal (forever) issues!

Our life here on planet earth is finite; it will end at some point. Therefore, the most important question is, “Where will you spend eternity?”

“Whereas ye know not what shall be on the morrow. For what is your life? It is even a vapour, that appeareth for a little time, and then vanisheth away” — James 4:14.

I Peter 1:24; Job 7:6-7; Job 14:1-2; Psalm 39:4-6; Psalm 89:47-48; Psalm 90:10; Luke 12:19-20; I Timothy 6:7.

This Bible study series will introduce you to the eternal SUPREME BEING (God), Who has spoken to all mankind through the eternal SCRIPTURES (the Bible), that tells us of God’s eternal SON (Jesus Christ), through Whom we can have an eternal SALVATION that saves and keeps our everlasting SOUL.

Bible Study Three

The Everlasting Soul (Man)

Key Verse: Genesis 2:7.

“And the LORD God formed man of the dust of the ground, and breathed into his nostrils the breath of life; and man became a LIVING SOUL.”

— The Creation of Man —

From the first two chapters of the book of Genesis we learn that the creation of man was:

  1. DELIBERATE — Genesis 1:26.

    “And God said, Let us make man [a deliberative choice] in our image, after our likeness: and let them have dominion over the fish of the sea, and over the fowl of the air, and over the cattle, and over all the earth, and over every creeping thing that creepeth upon the earth.”

    The very fact that God deliberately chose to create man means that we are special to Him. We are “wanted.”

  2. DISTINCT from the animal kingdom — Genesis 1:20-25.

    “And God said, Let the waters bring forth abundantly the moving creature that hath life, and fowl that may fly above the earth in the open firmament of heaven. And God created great whales, and every living creature that moveth, which the waters brought forth abundantly, after their kind, and every winged fowl after his kind: and God saw that it was good. And God blessed them, saying, Be fruitful, and multiply, and fill the waters in the seas, and let fowl multiply in the earth. And the evening and the morning were the fifth day. And God said, Let the earth bring forth the living creature after his kind, cattle, and creeping thing, and beast of the earth after his kind: and it was so. And God made the beast of the earth after his kind, and cattle after their kind, and every thing that creepeth upon the earth after his kind: and God saw that it was good.”

    1. Man’s creation was a separate event. (He was not included in the creation of aquatic, atmospheric, or terrestrial animal life.)
    2. Vegetation and animals can only reproduce after their “kind.”
      • They do not evolve from one species into another.

  3. DIFFERENT from animals:

    Man was created in the image and likeness of God — Genesis 1:27.

    “So God created man in his own image, in the image of God created he him; male and female created he them.”

    1. The “image of God” means man has THREE parts:

      1. The Godhead is plural — Genesis 1:26a-c. “And God said, Let US make man in OUR image, after our likeness…”
      2. One God, three Persons — I John 5:7. “For there are three that bear record in heaven, the Father, the Word, and the Holy Ghost: and these three are one.”
      3. Man is body, soul, and spirit — I Thessalonians 5:23.

        “And the very God of peace sanctify you wholly; and I pray God your whole spirit and soul and body be preserved blameless unto the coming of our Lord Jesus Christ.”

        • The body — sense-consciousness.
        • The soul — self-consciousness.
        • The spirit — God-consciousness.
        • Your soul and spirit are the “real you” — housed in a body of clay.

    2. The “likeness of God” means man has God’s CHARACTER:

      1. Creativity — the ability to make.
      2. Personality — the ability to relate.
      3. Intellect — the ability to think.
      4. Emotion — the ability to feel.
      5. Volition — the ability to choose.
    3. Animals were not created in God’s image or likeness. Anything they do that is humanlike is instinctive.

  4. DIRECT — Genesis 2:7.

    “And the LORD God formed man of the dust of the ground, and breathed into his nostrils the breath of life; and man became a living soul.”

    1. God spoke all plant and animal life into existence en masse.
    2. Man was “handcrafted” and “one off.”
    3. Man’s SPIRIT was given directly by the LORD — Job 33:4.

      “The Spirit of God hath made me, and the breath of the Almighty hath given me life.”

    4. Human life comes directly from God. Therefore:
      1. Human life is SACRED.
      2. Human life is EVERLASTING — it does not end.

    This is the answer to the first of life’s greatest questions: “Where did I come from?”

— The Purpose of Man —

The answer to the second of life’s greatest questions (“Why am I here?”) is seen in Genesis chapter 2. In a word, it is “relationship” — man was created to enjoy a personal relationship with his Creator.

  1. A PERSONAL relationship.

    1. God communicated personally with man — Genesis 2:16.

      “And the LORD God commanded the man, saying, Of every tree of the garden thou mayest freely eat.”

    2. God sought out man — Genesis 3:8-9.

      “And they heard the voice of the LORD God walking in the garden in the cool of the day: and Adam and his wife hid themselves from the presence of the LORD God amongst the trees of the garden. And the LORD God called unto Adam, and said unto him, Where art thou?”

    3. God desires a definite relationship — Isaiah 57:15.

      “For thus saith the high and lofty One that inhabiteth eternity, whose name is Holy; I dwell in the high and holy place, with him also that is of a contrite and humble spirit…”

      I John 1:3; Leviticus 26:12; II Corinthians 6:18; Isaiah 66:1-2.

    4. God desires an enjoyable relationship — Revelation 4:11.

      “Thou art worthy, O Lord, to receive glory and honour and power: for thou hast created all things, and for thy pleasure they are and were created.”

    5. Man is made for a relationship — Psalm 73:25.

      “Whom have I in heaven but thee? and there is none upon earth that I desire beside thee.”

  2. A BENEVOLENT relationship.

    1. God blessed man with abundant PROVISION — Genesis 2:8-9a.

      “And the LORD God planted a garden eastward in Eden; and there he put the man whom he had formed. And out of the ground made the LORD God to grow every tree that is pleasant to the sight, and good for food…”

    2. God blessed man with a PURPOSE — Genesis 1:28.

      “And God blessed them, and God said unto them, Be fruitful, and multiply, and replenish the earth, and subdue it: and have dominion over the fish of the sea, and over the fowl of the air, and over every living thing that moveth upon the earth.”

    3. God blessed man with a life PARTNER — Genesis 2:18,22.

      “And the LORD God said, It is not good that the man should be alone; I will make him an help meet for him ... And the rib, which the LORD God had taken from man, made he a woman, and brought her unto the man.”

  3. A FREE WILL relationship.

    Man was not created as a mindless robot or as an instinctive, impulsive animal.

    God created man with a free will so that we would deliberately choose to love Him and seek His presence. However, a free will cannot be a free will unless and until it is tested.

    1. God gave Adam a simple test — Genesis 2:16-17.

      “And the LORD God commanded the man, saying, Of every tree of the garden thou mayest freely eat: But of the tree of the knowledge of good and evil, thou shalt not eat of it: for in the day that thou eatest thereof thou shalt surely die.”

    2. This test came with a clear choice — to obey, or to disobey.
    3. The choice had consequences — disobedience brought DEATH.

— The Fall of Man —

Genesis chapter 3 describes man’s fall into sin and its aftermath:

  1. Satan worked through the serpent to beguile Eve — Genesis 3:1.

    “Now the serpent was more subtil than any beast of the field which the LORD God had made. And he said unto the woman, Yea, hath God said, Ye shall not eat of every tree of the garden?”

    II Corinthians 11:3.

  2. Eve was deceived; Adam disobeyed willfully — I Timothy 2:14. “And Adam was not deceived, but the woman being deceived was in the transgression.”
  3. Sin brought a CURSE upon man — Genesis 3:16-17.

    “Unto the woman he said, I will greatly multiply thy sorrow and thy conception; in sorrow thou shalt bring forth children; and thy desire shall be to thy husband, and he shall rule over thee. And unto Adam he said, Because thou hast hearkened unto the voice of thy wife, and hast eaten of the tree, of which I commanded thee, saying, Thou shalt not eat of it: CURSED is the ground for thy sake; in sorrow shalt thou eat of it all the days of thy life.”

  4. Sin brought a curse upon all creation — Genesis 3:17-18.

    “And unto Adam he said, Because thou hast hearkened unto the voice of thy wife, and hast eaten of the tree, of which I commanded thee, saying, Thou shalt not eat of it: cursed is the ground for thy sake; in sorrow shalt thou eat of it all the days of thy life; Thorns also and thistles shall it bring forth to thee; and thou shalt eat the herb of the field.”

    Romans 8:22.

  5. The penalty for sin is DEATH — Genesis 2:17.

    “But of the tree of the knowledge of good and evil, thou shalt not eat of it: for in the day that thou eatest thereof thou shalt surely DIE.”

    The word “death” means a separation (never annihilation). There are three kinds of death taught in the scriptures:

    1. PHYSICAL death:

      This is the separation of man’s soul and spirit from his body. It is the form of death we can observe.

      1. Genesis 3:19.

        “In the sweat of thy face shalt thou eat bread, till thou return unto the ground; for out of it wast thou taken: for dust thou art, and unto dust shalt thou return.”

      2. Genesis 5:5. “And all the days that Adam lived were nine hundred and thirty years: AND HE DIED.”
      3. Ecclesiastes 12:7.

        “Then shall the dust return to the earth as it was: and the spirit shall return unto God who gave it.”

      Genesis 35:18; John 19:30; II Corinthians 5:1.

      Because of Adam’s sin, the day will come when loved ones and friends will view your lifeless body laid out in a coffin.

    2. SPIRITUAL death:

      This is the separation of man’s soul and spirit (the “real you”) from God. Sin separates us from God while we are living.

      1. Sin resulted in an immediate separation (estrangement) of Adam and Eve from the Lord God — Genesis 3:8.

        “And they heard the voice of the LORD God walking in the garden in the cool of the day: and Adam and his wife hid themselves from the presence of the LORD God amongst the trees of the garden.”

      2. Isaiah 59:1-2.

        “Behold, the LORD’S hand is not shortened, that it cannot save; neither his ear heavy, that it cannot hear: But your iniquities have separated between you and your God, and your sins have hid his face from you, that he will not hear.”

      3. Ephesians 2:1. “And you hath he quickened [made alive], who were dead in trespasses and sins.”

    3. ETERNAL death:

      This is the separation of a man from his Creator FOREVER in the Lake of Fire.

      1. Matthew 25:41,46.

        “Then shall he say also unto them on the left hand, Depart from me, ye cursed, into everlasting fire, prepared for the devil and his angels … And these shall go away into everlasting punishment: but the righteous into life eternal.”

      2. Luke 16:22de-23ab. “…the rich man also died, and was buried; And in HELL he lift up his eyes, being in torments…”
      3. Revelation 20:14-15.

        “And death and hell were cast into the lake of fire. This is the second death. And whosoever was not found written in the book of life was cast into the lake of fire.”

      Revelation 19:20; Revelation 21:8; Mark 9:43-48.

— The Problem for Man —

  1. Adam’s sin nature was passed on to ALL men — Romans 5:12.

    “Wherefore, as by one man [Adam] sin entered into the world, and death by sin; and so death passed upon ALL men, for that all have sinned.”

    1. Adam and Eve’s first child committed murder — Genesis 4:8.

      “And Cain talked with Abel his brother: and it came to pass, when they were in the field, that Cain rose up against Abel his brother, and slew him.”

    2. By the time of Noah, man’s wickedness was pervasive — Genesis 6:5.

      “And GOD saw that the wickedness of man was great in the earth, and that every imagination of the thoughts of his heart was only evil continually.”

    3. All men are sinners by virtue of their connection to Adam:

      1. Romans 3:10. “As it is written, There is none righteous, no, not one.”
      2. Romans 3:23. “For all have sinned, and come short of the glory of God.” Ecclesiastes 7:20; Romans 3:12.
    4. Even babies sin — Psalm 58:3. “The wicked are estranged from the womb: they go astray as soon as they be born, speaking lies.”

    5. We are all sinners:

      1. By birth — Psalm 51:5. “Behold, I was shapen in iniquity; and in sin did my mother conceive me.”
      2. By nature — Jeremiah 17:9. “The heart is deceitful above all things, and desperately wicked: who can know it?”
      3. By choice — John 3:19. “And this is the condemnation, that light is come into the world, and men loved darkness rather than light, because their deeds were evil.”
      4. By deed — Romans 1:32. “Who knowing the judgment of God, that they which commit such things are worthy of death, not only do the same, but have pleasure in them that do them.”

  2. Sin has consequences — Romans 6:23a. “For the wages of sin is DEATH...”

    This means:

    1. Spiritual death (now) — no relationship or fellowship with God; no spiritual life — Ephesians 4:18.

      “Having the understanding darkened, being alienated from the life of God through the ignorance that is in them, because of the blindness of their heart.”

    2. Physical death (soon) — Hebrews 9:27. “And as it is appointed unto men once to die, but after this the judgment.”
    3. The Second death (eternity) — Revelation 21:27.

      “And there shall in no wise enter into it [Heaven] any thing that defileth, neither whatsoever worketh abomination, or maketh a lie: but they which are written in the Lamb's book of life.”

      Revelation 20:14.

  3. We are all without excuse:

    1. God codified and defined sin in His holy LAW — Romans 7:7.

      “What shall we say then? Is the law sin? God forbid. Nay, I had not known sin, but by the law: for I had not known lust, except the law had said, Thou shalt not covet.”

    2. Nine of the “Ten Commandments” encapsulate the moral laws of God.

      — At this point, use the “God’s Standard” study —

    3. The Law is the standard or measure by which we are judged. This standard is actually God Himself! — Matthew 5:48. “Be ye therefore perfect, even as your Father which is in heaven is perfect.”
    4. Jesus gave us a summation of the Law — Matthew 22:37-40.

      “Jesus said unto him, Thou shalt love the Lord thy God with ALL thy heart, and with ALL thy soul, and with ALL thy mind. This is the first and great commandment. And the second is like unto it, Thou shalt love thy neighbour as thyself. On these two commandments hang all the law and the prophets.”

    5. Have you obeyed this perfectly throughout your whole life? Yes? No?
    6. ONE violation of God’s Law makes us “guilty?” — James 2:10.

      “For whosoever shall keep the whole law, and yet offend in one point, he is guilty of all.”

    7. We are, in fact, totally DEFILED by sin:

      1. Mark 7:21-23 — “For from within, out of the heart of men, proceed evil thoughts, adulteries, fornications, murders, Thefts, covetousness, wickedness, deceit, lasciviousness, an evil eye, blasphemy, pride, foolishness: All these evil things come from within, and defile the man.”
      2. Revelation 21:27 — “And there shall in no wise enter into it [Heaven] any thing that defileth, neither whatsoever worketh abomination, or maketh a lie: but they which are written in the Lamb's book of life.”

      — At this point, use the “40 Sins” study —

    8. All sin is first and foremost against GOD — Psalm 51:3-4.

      “For I acknowledge my transgressions: and my sin is ever before me. Against thee, thee only, have I sinned, and done this evil in thy sight: that thou mightest be justified when thou speakest, and be clear when thou judgest.”

      David’s sin here was the one he committed with Bathsheba.

  4. We are all under condemnation — Romans 5:18a. “Therefore as by the offence of one [Adam] judgment came upon all men to condemnation…” John 3:18-19.

    1. God, not man, is the Judge — Romans 2:1-3.

      “Therefore thou art inexcusable, O man, whosoever thou art that judgest: for wherein thou judgest another, thou condemnest thyself; for thou that judgest doest the same things. But we are sure that the judgment of God is according to truth against them which commit such things. And thinkest thou this, O man, that judgest them which do such things, and doest the same, that thou shalt escape the judgment of God?”

    2. The VERDICT can only be “Guilty as charged!” — Romans 3:19.

      “Now we know that what things soever the law saith, it saith to them who are under the law: that every mouth may be stopped, and ALL the world may become guilty before God.”

— Summary —

  1. You were designed and created by God to enjoy a personal relationship with Him — Romans 8:14-17.

    “For as many as are led by the Spirit of God, they are the SONS of God. For ye have not received the spirit of bondage again to fear; but ye have received the Spirit of adoption, whereby we cry, ABBA, FATHER. The Spirit itself beareth witness with our spirit, that we are the CHILDREN of God: And if children, then HEIRS; heirs of God, and joint-heirs with Christ; if so be that we suffer with him, that we may be also glorified together.”

  2. Adam’s sin broke that relationship — Romans 5:19a. “For as by one man's disobedience many were made sinners…”
  3. Adam’s sin nature (and its consequences) was passed down to ALL men — I Corinthians 15:22a. “For as in Adam all die…”
  4. God’s holiness demands that ALL sin be punished — Psalm 5:4-5.

    “For thou art not a God that hath pleasure in wickedness: neither shall evil dwell with thee. The foolish shall not stand in thy sight: thou hatest all workers of iniquity.”

  5. The ultimate penalty for sin is for your everlasting soul (the “real you”) to spend forever in the Lake of Fire — Psalm 9:17. “The wicked shall be turned into HELL, and all the nations that forget God.”
