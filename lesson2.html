Bible Study Two

The Eternal Scriptures (The Bible)

Key Verse: Psalm 119:160.

“Thy word is true from the beginning: and every one of thy righteous judgments endureth for ever.”

— Introduction —

  1. God is a COMMUNICATOR:

    1. I John 4:8b — “God is love.”
      1. Love means the unconditional giving of oneself to another.
      2. One cannot love without communicating — both in word and in deed.
    2. The object of God’s love is MAN — Psalm 8:3-5.

      “When I consider thy heavens, the work of thy fingers, the moon and the stars, which thou hast ordained; What is man, that thou art mindful of him? and the son of man, that thou visitest him? For thou hast made him a little lower than the angels, and hast crowned him with glory and honour.”

    3. The first communication between God and man came from GOD — Genesis 2:15-16.

      “And the LORD God took the man, and put him into the garden of Eden to dress it and to keep it, And the LORD God commanded the man, saying, Of every tree of the garden thou mayest freely eat.”

    4. God’s communication with man is called “revelation,” or a revealing. If God didn’t reveal Himself, we would never know that there was a God — let alone what He is like and what He has done. Look up: Deuteronomy 29:29.

      “The secret things belong unto the LORD our God: but those things which are revealed belong unto us and to our children for ever, that we may do all the words of this law.”

    5. All God’s revelation of Himself is TRUTH — Deuteronomy 32:4.

      “He is the Rock, his work is perfect: for all his ways are judgment: a God of truth and without iniquity, just and right is he.”

  2. God communicates with man in TWO ways:

    1. In a general way through His CREATION — Psalm 19:1-3.

      “The heavens declare the glory of God; and the firmament sheweth his handywork. Day unto day uttereth speech, and night unto night sheweth knowledge. There is no speech nor language, where their voice is not heard.”

      Romans 1:19-20; Romans 2:14-15.

    2. In a special way through the SCRIPTURES — Ephesians 3:3-5.

      “How that by revelation he made known unto me the mystery; (as I wrote afore in few words, Whereby, when ye read, ye may understand my knowledge in the mystery of Christ) Which in other ages was not made known unto the sons of men, as it is now revealed unto his holy apostles and prophets by the Spirit.”

      I Corinthians 2:9-10; Galatians 1:11-12.

    The Bible is a “love gift” from God to man. It is the way God communicates and reveals His will to us — written down so that all men everywhere and in every age can hear Him.

    Jesus said:

    • John 8:32 — “And ye shall know the truth, and the truth shall make you free.”
    • John 17:17 — “Sanctify them through thy truth: thy word is truth.”

    This Bible study will help you understand exactly what the Bible is, why it can be trusted and believed, and why it is so essential to experiencing a personal relationship with God.

— The Bible Is a Supernatural Book —

Although it has the characteristics of most other books (words, pages, chapters, covers, etc.), the Holy Bible is in fact a supernatural book.

Why?

  1. Because of the manner in which it was GIVEN.

    1. The Bible was originally given from God by a process known as “inspiration” — II Timothy 3:16.

      “All scripture is given by inspiration of God, and is profitable for doctrine, for reproof, for correction, for instruction in righteousness.”

    2. The process of inspiration simply means that GOD revealed truth and had MAN write it down (without error or change) — II Peter 1:21.

      “For the prophecy came not in old time by the will of man: but holy men of God spake as they were moved by the Holy Ghost.”

      II Samuel 23:1; Mark 12:36; Numbers 16:28; Luke 1:70.

  2. Because of the way in which it is PRESERVED.

    The revelation of truth from God to man was supernaturally written down by about 40 different men over a period of some 1,600 years. This written record made it possible for God’s Word to be transmitted (reproduced) for the generations to come.

    1. Ordinarily, one might expect the copying of the original writings of God’s prophets (by many human hands over many centuries of time) would result in scribal mistakes, allowing for errors to creep into the Word of God. However …
    2. God promised to preserve His Word — Psalm 12:6-7.

      “The words of the LORD are pure words: as silver tried in a furnace of earth, purified seven times. Thou shalt keep them, O LORD, thou shalt preserve them from this generation for ever.”

      Psalm 33:11; Psalm 100:5,8; Matthew 24:35.

    3. With the eternal God as their Source, the Holy Scriptures are eternal — Psalm 119:89.

      “For ever, O LORD, thy word is settled in heaven.”

      Isaiah 40:8; I Peter 1:23,25.

      1. God’s Word existed in eternity PAST — Titus 1:2. “In hope of eternal life, which God, that cannot lie, promised before the world began.”
      2. God’s Word will still exist in the eternal FUTURE — John 12:48. “He that rejecteth me, and receiveth not my words, hath one that judgeth him: the word that I have spoken, the same shall judge him in the last day.” Revelation 20:11-12.
    4. For the English-speaking world, God has preserved His Word in the Authorized, King James Version. It was produced in 1611.
    5. Satan’s first communication with man was to question and contradict what God had said — Genesis 3:1.

      “Now the serpent was more subtil than any beast of the field which the LORD God had made. And he said unto the woman, Yea, hath God said, Ye shall not eat of every tree of the garden?”

      John 8:44.

    6. Satan has continually sought to keep God’s truth from mankind. He does so today primarily through the many “versions” and “translations” being produced, none of which say exactly the same thing, let alone agree with the King James Bible — II Corinthians 2:14.

      “For we are not as many, which corrupt the word of God: but as of sincerity, but as of God, in the sight of God speak we in Christ.”

— The Bible Is a Credible Book —

Many refuse to accept the Bible as God’s Word. They think it was written only by men and is full of contradictions and inaccuracies (although they never seem to be able to find any).

Relationships are built on trust. If you are to have a personal relationship with your Creator, you must be able to trust what He says.

  1. You can trust the Bible because it is in harmony with natural laws.

    Although it is not a scientific textbook, the Bible does harmonize with the established laws of science. Why? Because the Bible was given by the One Who created the natural world.

    A couple of examples:

    1. The genetic unity of the human race — Acts 17:24-26.

      “God that made the world and all things therein, seeing that he is Lord of heaven and earth, dwelleth not in temples made with hands; Neither is worshipped with men's hands, as though he needed any thing, seeing he giveth to all life, and breath, and all things; And hath made of one blood all nations of men for to dwell on all the face of the earth, and hath determined the times before appointed, and the bounds of their habitation.”

    2. The genetic dissimilarity of humans and animals — I Corinthians 15:39.

      “All flesh is not the same flesh: but there is one kind of flesh of men, another flesh of beasts, another of fishes, and another of birds.”

  2. You can trust the Bible because of the two greatest proofs of its credibility:

    1. ARCHÆOLOGY — proofs from the outside:

      The renowned Jewish archæologist Nelson Glueck (1900-1971) wrote: “It may be stated categorically that no archæological discovery has ever controverted a biblical reference.”

      A couple of examples:

      1. I Kings 9:15-17.

        “And this is the reason of the levy which king Solomon raised; for to build the house of the LORD, and his own house, and Millo, and the wall of Jerusalem, and Hazor, and Megiddo, and Gezer. For Pharaoh king of Egypt had gone up, and taken Gezer, and burnt it with fire…”

        Excavations in the latter-half of the 20TH century uncovered Solomonic-style walls and gates at Hazor, Megiddo, and Gezer — and evidence of an Egyptian destruction at Gezer.

      2. Daniel 5:1,30-31.

        “Belshazzar the king made a great feast to a thousand of his lords, and drank wine before the thousand … In that night was Belshazzar the king of the Chaldeans slain. And Darius the Median took the kingdom, being about threescore and two years old.”

        The historical monuments all declare Nabonidus was the last king of Babylon — a fact that had long given much “ammunition” to the critics of the Bible (which clearly states that Belshazzar was its last king). However, in 1881, a cuneiform inscription now known as the “Nabonidus Cylinder” was discovered in Iraq. This cylinder mentions a Bel-shar-usur (Belshazzar) by name, and identifies him as the son of Nabonidus. Evidently, Nabonidus had left the city of Babylon to fight the Medes and Persians, leaving Belshazzar in charge. This explains why the BIBLE records Belshazzar offering to make Daniel the third ruler in the kingdom (not the second) — Daniel 5:16.

      Many other examples can be cited if needed. However, do not lose the focus of this study.

    2. FULFILLED PROPHECY — proofs from the inside:

      When the Bible was first written down, approximately 75% of it was prophetic. Since then, numerous detailed prophecies have been fulfilled with great accuracy.

      Messianic prophecy alone is remarkable. Over 300 prophecies of the birth, ministry, passion, resurrection, and ascension of Christ have already been fulfilled. In one 24 hour period (the day of Christ’s crucifixion), approximately 21 detailed prophecies were fulfilled. The statistical “odds” of this being mere chance is staggering. (The chance of one man accidently fulfilling just eight prophecies is 1 in 1017!)

      A couple of examples:

      1. Jesus betrayed for 30 pieces of silver — Zechariah 11:12-13. (Prophesied c.520 B.C.)

        “And I said unto them, If ye think good, give me my price; and if not, forbear. So they weighed for my price thirty pieces of silver. And the LORD said unto me, Cast it unto the potter: a goodly price that I was prised at of them. And I took the thirty pieces of silver, and cast them to the potter in the house of the LORD.”

        Fulfilment:

        • Matthew 26:14-15 — “Then one of the twelve, called Judas Iscariot, went unto the chief priests, And said unto them, What will ye give me, and I will deliver him unto you? And they covenanted with him for thirty pieces of silver.”
        • Matthew 27:3-7 — Then Judas, which had betrayed him, when he saw that he was condemned, repented himself, and brought again the thirty pieces of silver to the chief priests and elders, Saying, I have sinned in that I have betrayed the innocent blood. And they said, What is that to us? see thou to that. And he cast down the pieces of silver in the temple, and departed, and went and hanged himself. And the chief priests took the silver pieces, and said, It is not lawful for to put them into the treasury, because it is the price of blood. And they took counsel, and bought with them the potter’s field, to bury strangers in.”

      2. Jesus crucified — Psalm 22:14-18. (Prophesied c.1060 B.C.)

        “I am poured out like water, and all my bones are out of joint: my heart is like wax; it is melted in the midst of my bowels. My strength is dried up like a potsherd; and my tongue cleaveth to my jaws; and thou hast brought me into the dust of death. For dogs have compassed me: the assembly of the wicked have inclosed me: they pierced my hands and my feet. I may tell all my bones: they look and stare upon me. They part my garments among them, and cast lots upon my vesture.”

        Fulfillment:

        • Matthew 27:35 — “And they crucified him, and parted his garments, casting lots: that it might be fulfilled which was spoken by the prophet, They parted my garments among them, and upon my vesture did they cast lots.”

      Many other examples can be cited if needed. However, do not lose the focus of this study.

3. Because the Bible is trustworthy, it is AUTHORITATIVE:

  1. Truth is ABSOLUTE — it is not changeable or relative.

    1. To explain:
      • 1 + 1 = 2 (always).
      • It can never be 3 (the answer is not changeable).
      • It can never be what you think it should be at any given time (relativism).
    2. Truth is absolute because its Source is GOD.
      • Hebrews 6:17-19 — “Wherein God, willing more abundantly to shew unto the heirs of promise the immutability of his counsel, confirmed it by an oath: That by two immutable things, in which it was impossible for God to lie, we might have a strong consolation, who have fled for refuge to lay hold upon the hope set before us: Which hope we have as an anchor of the soul, both sure and stedfast, and which entereth into that within the veil” Titus 1:2; Numbers 23:19; Romans 3:4.
      • Malachi 3:6ab — “For I am the LORD, I change not.”

  2. Truth is EXCLUSIVE — there cannot be two opposite “truths.”

    1. Colossians 2:8 — “Beware lest any man spoil you through philosophy and vain deceit, after the tradition of men, after the rudiments of the world, and not after Christ.”
    2. I Timothy 1:3-4 — “As I besought thee to abide still at Ephesus, when I went into Macedonia, that thou mightest charge some that they teach no other doctrine, Neither give heed to fables and endless genealogies, which minister questions, rather than godly edifying which is in faith: so do.”
    3. Titus 1:14 — “Not giving heed to Jewish fables, and commandments of men, that turn from the truth.” Matthew 15:9; Colossians 2:22; II Timothy 2:16,23; Titus 3:9.

    Truth does not come from man. The creeds, traditions, and doctrines of religious organizations are truthful only inasmuch as they agree with God’s Word. The same goes for any man’s opinions or religious experiences.

  3. Truth is KNOWABLE and TESTABLE (it is possible to know what is right and what is wrong) — I Thessalonians 5:21. “Prove all things; hold fast that which is good.” Acts 17:11; II Timothy 2:15; Isaiah 8:20.

  4. Truth is UNDENIABLE. Once it is known it cannot be un-known! Truth, once seen, can only be obeyed or disobeyed; accepted or rejected — James 1:22. “But be ye doers of the word, and not hearers only, deceiving your own selves.” John 12:48; James 4:17.

  5. The Bible is the only absolute and final authority in all matters of faith and practice — Deuteronomy 4:2. “Ye shall not add unto the word which I command you, neither shall ye diminish ought from it, that ye may keep the commandments of the LORD your God which I command you.” Proverbs 30:6; Revelation 22:18.

  6. In the Bible, God has given us everything we need to know.

    1. II Peter 1:3-4 — “According as his divine power hath given unto us ALL things that pertain unto life and godliness, through the knowledge of him that hath called us to glory and virtue: Whereby are given unto us exceeding great and precious promises [God’s Word]: that by these ye might be partakers of the divine nature, having escaped the corruption that is in the world through lust.”
    2. II Timothy 3:15-17 — “And that from a child thou hast known the holy scriptures, which are able to make thee wise unto salvation through faith which is in Christ Jesus. All scripture is given by inspiration of God, and is profitable for doctrine, for reproof, for correction, for instruction in righteousness: That the man of God may be perfect, throughly furnished unto ALL good works.”

    There is no need to look elsewhere. You can trust the Bible!
