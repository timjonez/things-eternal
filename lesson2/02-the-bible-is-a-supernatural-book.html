— The Bible Is a Supernatural Book —

Although it has the characteristics of most other books (words, pages, chapters, covers, etc.), the Holy Bible is in fact a supernatural book.

Why?

  1. Because of the manner in which it was GIVEN.

    1. The Bible was originally given from God by a process known as “inspiration” —

      II Timothy 3:16 — “All ___ is given by inspiration of God, and is profitable for doctrine, for reproof, for ___, for instruction in righteousness.”

    2. The process of inspiration simply means that GOD revealed truth and had MAN write it down (without error or change) —

      II Peter 1:21 — “For the ___ came not in old time by the will of man: but holy men of God spake as they were moved by the ___ Ghost.”

      II Samuel 23:1II Samuel 23:1 — Now these be the last words of David. David the son of Jesse said, and the man who was raised up on high, the anointed of the God of Jacob, and the sweet psalmist of Israel, said,; Mark 12:36Mark 12:36 — For David himself said by the Holy Ghost, The LORD said to my Lord, Sit thou on my right hand, till I make thine enemies thy footstool.; Numbers 16:28Numbers 16:28 — And Moses said, Hereby ye shall know that the LORD hath sent me to do all these works; for I have not done them of mine own mind.; Luke 1:70Luke 1:70 — As he spake by the mouth of his holy prophets, which have been since the world began:.

  2. Because of the way in which it is PRESERVED.

    The revelation of truth from God to man was supernaturally written down by about 40 different men over a period of some 1,600 years. This written record made it possible for God’s Word to be transmitted (reproduced) for the generations to come.

    1. Ordinarily, one might expect the copying of the original writings of God’s prophets (by many human hands over many centuries of time) would result in scribal mistakes, allowing for errors to creep into the Word of God. However …
    2. God promised to preserve His Word —

      Psalm 12:6-7 — “The ___ of the LORD are pure words: as silver tried in a furnace of ___, purified seven times. Thou shalt ___ them, O LORD, thou shalt preserve them from this generation for ever.”

    3. With the eternal God as their Source, the Holy Scriptures are eternal —

      Psalm 119:89 — “For ever, O ___, thy word is settled in heaven.”

      Isaiah 40:8; I Peter 1:23,25.

      1. God’s Word existed in eternity PAST —

        Titus 1:2 — “In hope of eternal life, which God, that cannot lie, promised before the world began.”

      2. God’s Word will still exist in the eternal FUTURE —

        John 12:48 — “He that rejecteth me, and receiveth not my words, hath one that judgeth him: the word that I have spoken, the same shall judge him in the last day.”

        Revelation 20:11-12.

    5. Satan’s first communication with man was to question and contradict what God had said —

      Genesis 3:1 — “Now the ___ was more subtil than any beast of the field which the LORD God had made. And he said unto the woman, Yea, hath God said, Ye shall not eat of ___ tree of the garden?”
